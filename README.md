# Using Claude as a Strategic Advisor

A practical guide you can follow right now, step by step.

## How to use this

Work through each section in order — paste the prompts directly into Claude as you go. Each step builds on the previous one. Budget 60–90 minutes for the first session.

**What you need:**
- Claude Pro (claude.ai)
- One real problem or goal you want to work through

---

## Step 1: Create a Project

Go to **claude.ai → Projects → New Project**.

Name it after whatever you're working on right now — `Prospecting`, `Q3 Strategy`, `New Offer`, etc.

> **Why bother with a project?** Without one, every chat starts cold. A project gives Claude running context across sessions. You can also upload reference files (company overview, pricing, sample proposals) that every new chat inside the project can see automatically — no more copy-pasting the same background every time.

Do this now, then open a new chat inside the project to continue.

---

## Step 2: Build a Master File (15 min)

Claude can't give you useful advice until it knows who you are and what you're working on. Paste this to kick off the interview:

```
I want to build a "Master File" — a short document that gives you permanent context about me and my business.

Interview me to gather the info. Ask one question at a time. Cover:
- My name and company
- What we sell and who we sell to
- Our main competitive advantage
- The biggest challenge or goal I'm working on right now
- What success looks like in the next 90 days

After 6–8 questions, generate a structured Master File. I'll correct anything that's off.
```

When Claude generates the document, click the artifact icon → **Add to project**.

From here on, every chat in this project starts with that context already loaded.

> **Don't worry about being polished.** Answer in stream-of-consciousness. Change your mind mid-sentence. Claude produces clean output from messy input. More often than not, just answering the questions clarifies your thinking better than writing a document ever would.

---

## Step 3: Define Your Ideal Customer Profile

Open a new chat inside the project. Paste this:

```
You are an experienced B2B sales consultant. Help me define my Ideal Customer Profile (ICP).

Interview me about my best clients. Ask one focused question at a time. After 6–8 questions, synthesize what you've learned into a structured ICP covering:
- Industry and company type
- Job title of the buyer and key influencer
- Their specific pain point
- What triggers them to start looking for a solution
- What makes them disqualify vendors
- What a successful deal looks like

Start with: Who was your single best client, and what made them the best?
```

Answer honestly. When Claude produces the ICP summary, add it to the project.

> **Why the interview format?** Most founders describe their ICP from memory and end up with something vague. Having Claude ask you specific questions — one at a time — surfaces details you wouldn't have thought to write down. One founder described it as: *"It's like a workshop. It asks questions I wouldn't have asked myself."*

---

## Step 4: Build Your Outreach Strategy

Open a new chat and type:

```
Based on my ICP and Master File (in the project files), how should I find and reach these clients?

Additional context: [add 1–2 sentences about your specific situation — e.g. "My total market is about 6 companies. LinkedIn outreach hasn't worked — my buyers are technical engineers, not marketing directors."]
```

Read the response critically. Claude will give you a framework — your job is to push back where it doesn't fit your reality:

- "That won't work because..."
- "We tried that — here's what happened..."
- "What if the market is too small for that approach?"

> **Claude doesn't know your strategy — you do.** Claude executes well but needs your judgment at the strategic level. If your market is under 10 accounts, say so explicitly. The approach for 6 accounts looks completely different from a 600-account campaign. When your whole market is 6 companies: referrals, not funnels. One relationship, not a sequence.

---

## Step 5: Research Specific Leads

In the same chat, enable **Web Search** (the globe icon in the input bar). Then:

```
Find [specific role] at [specific company type or geography].

Focus on people who would [describe what they do that's relevant to you — e.g. "write technical specifications for gas analyzer installations" or "sign off on instrumentation purchases"].
```

The more specific the thread you give Claude to pull, the more useful the results. Generic questions produce generic answers.

Follow up with:

```
For each person you found: given that they probably aren't active on LinkedIn or in marketing databases, what's the most realistic way to reach them?
```

---

## Step 6: Save What You Learned

Before closing any productive session, run this:

```
Summarize everything we've covered:
- Key decisions or conclusions
- Open questions still to resolve
- My 2–3 concrete next actions

Generate this as a structured artifact and add it to the project.
```

Takes 30 seconds. The next session starts at a higher baseline instead of from scratch.

## Ongoing Habits

The real value comes from short daily use, not big one-off sessions.

**Before a meeting:** "I have a call with [person] about [topic]. What should I make sure to cover? What questions should I ask?"

**When stuck on writing:** Ask for a draft. Reading a draft — even a mediocre one — clarifies what you actually want to say. Write your version, then ask Claude to find the weak spots.

**When you have an idea:** Tell Claude. Ask what it thinks. You don't have to act on its reaction — but you'll make a better call having heard one.

**When something isn't working:** Don't just try harder. Paste your results and ask: "I've been doing X for [timeframe] and getting Y. What would you change?"

---

## Customize Claude's Behavior

In your project's **Instructions** field (visible from the project page), add:

```
Reply concisely. Skip compliments and filler phrases. Use bullet points for lists. If I ask a vague question, ask one clarifying question before answering.
```

Adjust to your taste. Claude applies this to every chat in the project.

---

## The Core Insight

Claude is only as useful as the context you give it. A brilliant advisor who knows nothing about your business gives generic advice. An average advisor who knows your customers, your constraints, and your history gives advice you can act on.

Build the context once — Master File, ICP, saved session artifacts. Then every conversation starts further along, and the compounding begins.
